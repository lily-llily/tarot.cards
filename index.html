Major Arcana

?
The Major Arcana: These are the "keys to unlocking life's mysteries." They represent major life themes, spiritual lessons, and karmic influences that transcend the daily elemental energy of the suits.
0 The Fool
The Fool: Card Zero. Represents a fresh start or new chapter without ties to the past. It's the ability to break out of thought patterns or toxic cycles and be reborn. Warning: Preparation is key; don't be reckless without understanding the consequences.
I The Magician
The Magician: The master manifester. Shows that you have all the tools (Cups, Swords, Wands, Pentacles) at your disposal. Success comes through alignment and realizing that your thoughts directly shape your physical reality.
II High Priestess
High Priestess: The Secret Keeper. Represents the duality between the conscious and subconscious. Master of "Shadow Work." She urges you to look deeper into signs and synchronicities, as hidden knowledge is currently at play.
III The Empress
The Empress: Embodiment of feminine energy and the season of spring. Represents growth, fertility, and new life. It's a nudge to embrace your Creative Energy, pay attention to physical health, and allow projects to grow naturally.
IV The Emperor
The Emperor: Control and command. Where the Empress wants things to grow wild, the Emperor provides direction. He warns you to put your foot down and re-establish structure before a situation gets out of hand.
V The Hierophant
The Hierophant: The link between the physical and spiritual. Represents institutions, books, and formal knowledge. It suggests that a spiritual routine or seeking traditional wisdom from a mentor or bureaucratic document is beneficial now.
VI The Lovers
The Lovers: Not just about romance; it's about moral choices and resisting temptation. It represents the choices we make to maintain our "Paradise." You are urged to make a decision aligned with your highest integrity.
VII The Chariot
The Chariot: Mastery over internal dualities (light/dark, yin/yang). Represents victory and raising your vibration through self-actualization. Things are moving forward and upward through sheer willpower and self-knowledge.
VIII Strength
Strength: Mastering your animalistic nature. It's about being "friends" with your wilder side rather than locking it away. This card indicates success achieved through integrity, inner willpower, and self-acceptance.
IX The Hermit
The Hermit: Reconnecting with your soul. Urges you to take time for yourself to think and feel without external influence. The lantern shows that even if you feel lost, the light within you will guide the way.
X Wheel of Fortune
Wheel of Fortune: Fate, Karma, and the Ebb and Flow. Represents a chapter ending and a new one starting. The universe is course-correcting; stay adaptable as the wheel turns from good to bad or back again.
XI Justice
Justice: Blind and balanced decision-making. You must step back from emotions and use logic. The outcome will be the "best" for the long run, even if it feels difficult in the moment.
XII The Hanged Man
Hanged Man: The Martyr. Represents standing by your convictions even if it means sacrifice. It signals that energy has slowed to a crawl to give you time to think and come to a better, more aligned conclusion.
XIII Death
Death: A natural conclusion and transition. Not a surprise, but an organic end to a season. Hanging on will cause pain; allowing it to run its course will usher in an inevitable and necessary rebirth.
XIV Temperance
Temperance: Mastery of balance. Similar to the High Priestess, but focused on the active alignment of the conscious and subconscious. Urges you to seek moderation and groundedness to allow the situation to stabilize.
XV The Devil
The Devil: Binding energy. Represents our "Shadow Side"—urges, addictions, or toxic cycles that keep us trapped. It calls for you to understand your shadow so you can relinquish its control over you.
XVI The Tower
The Tower: Sudden upheaval. Unlike the slow end of Death, this is an unforeseen collapse. It happens because the foundation was misaligned with your path; the universe is tearing it down to course-correct you.
XVII The Star
The Star: Healing and optimism. This is the peace that follows the Tower's destruction. Represents deep healing, presence, and a clear path toward an optimistic future. You are energetically "set free."
XVIII The Moon
The Moon: Illusions and mystery. Moonlight is reflected, not direct—meaning not everything is as it seems. Don't try to figure it out now; embrace the mystery and allow your psychic messages to pass through organically.
XIX The Sun
The Sun: Ultimate clarity and joy. The blockage is passing. It represents movement away from a wall toward happiness. Stay humble so the light doesn't blind you, but embrace the radiant success incoming.
XX Judgement
Judgement: Rising to the occasion. It's about changing your vibration and answering a call. You are being asked to understand what is best for you and take the necessary steps to move forward energetically.
XXI The World
The World: The final culmination. Success, spiritual mastery, and total fulfillment in a situation. You've reached the end of a long journey, and now the cycle is ready to reset to zero.

Suit of Wands

?
Wands (Fire Energy): Action, inspiration, and willpower. This suit is about the things we "do" and our personal drive to create and move forward. (Signs: Leo, Aries, Sagittarius)
Ace of Wands
Ace: A new spark of inspiration, a new project, or physical sexuality. Represents fire energy reaching out to you—seize the opportunity before it passes you by.
Two of Wands
Two: A door opening to a new adventure. You are making the decision to embark on a venture based on your own will. An omen to keep going and commit to your path.
Three of Wands
Three: Mastering and understanding the path you've chosen. You are becoming more established and master of your journey; no need to turn back now.
Four of Wands
Four: Stability, celebration, and the home. Represents a significant homecoming or a reason to celebrate with those in your immediate circle.
Five of Wands
Five: Conflict caused by fire energy. This struggle is often internal or caused by your own choices. It’s a sign that the universe is teaching you something through this blockage.
Six of Wands
Six: Victory and accomplishment. Public acknowledgment of your success is likely. An extremely positive card predicting a winning outcome for your current project.
Seven of Wands
Seven: External pressure. Unlike the 5, this is a struggle from the outside. You must decide if fighting for your beliefs is worth the energy or if you should retreat.
Eight of Wands
Eight: Rapid movement. Things are taking off faster than expected. Could also represent quick communication, an incoming surprise, or a sudden change in direction.
Nine of Wands
Nine: Resilience. You may be worn out, but you are still standing. Deciding whether to step back for your health or finally step out and show yourself to the world.
Ten of Wands
Ten: Over-responsibility. You are carrying a burden that is far too heavy. Question if you really need to be responsible for all these circumstances or if you should lighten the load.
Page of Wands
Page: Experimentation and play. A young spirit of "doing." It's time to try out your ideas in the real world and apply your passion practically.
Knight of Wands
Knight: Bold action and charging forward. However, it can be flaky energy—make sure you are actually committed to what you are jumping into.
Queen of Wands
Queen: Magnetic, confident, and sexually charged. She speaks up for herself but keeps her secrets. A master of attraction and self-actualization.
King of Wands
King: The master of fire. Executes plans with precision and success. He knows when to burn something down so that it can regrow even better than before.

Suit of Swords

?
Swords (Air Energy): Intellect, the human mind, psychology, and communication. This suit deals with our thoughts, perceptions, and the conflicts that arise within our heads. (Signs: Gemini, Aquarius, Libra)
Ace of Swords
Ace: New perspectives and breakthroughs. An opportunity for a "cut through the fog." Look for news, things to read, or conversations that provoke a change in mindset.
Two of Swords
Two: Decision-making. You may be blind to the truth right now. You are urged to make a choice using your mind rather than just what you think you see. Don't let indecision stall you.
Three of Swords
Three: Heartbreak and sorrow. Signals that current actions are leading to hurt. Your perception may be altered by the pain you feel—take time to care for your heart center first.
Four of Swords
Four: Rest and respite. After the heartbreak, you need meditation and a clear mind. Sharpen your mental faculty by stepping away from worries and simply breathing.
Five of Swords
Five: External conflict and arguing. You might win the argument, but at what cost? Is proving you are "right" worth hurting others or making the situation worse?
Six of Swords
Six: Transition. Slow movement away from a heavy situation. Even if you aren't fully healed, you must move away to start the recovery process and find a new scenery.
Seven of Swords
Seven: Making a getaway. Could be an urge to "get the F out" of a situation or a warning about deceit and stolen property. Check if you are overlooking something that could bite you later.
Eight of Swords
Eight: Mental claustrophobia. You feel trapped and confused, but the blindfold and binds are only your own thoughts. You can break free the moment you choose a new perspective.
Nine of Swords
Nine: Anxiety and nightmares. The suit of swords at its darkest—being tormented by your own thoughts at night. Step back and realize these fears only exist in your mind.
Ten of Swords
Ten: Ultimate defeat. Betrayal and endings that feel heavy (like Julius Caesar). However, the sun rising in the background shows that a new beginning is already dawning from this end.
Page of Swords
Page: New messages and news of change. A surprise conversation or an email that will shift your perspective. A young, curious energy seeking the truth.
Knight of Swords
Knight: Swift expression. You are urged to stop holding back and send that text or email. Be careful, though—rush communication can sometimes lead to regret.
Queen of Swords
Queen: Objective intellect. She has no problem making a decision but can be isolated. Urges you to make a clear choice without letting emotions cloud the "best" path.
King of Swords
King: The master of domain. He makes the right decision regardless of popular opinion. Wiser and authoritative, he does what is necessary to make things correct and right.

Suit of Cups

?
Cups (Water Energy): Emotions, relationships, joy, and the heart. This suit represents the things we "feel" and our interpersonal connections with others and ourselves. (Signs: Scorpio, Pisces, Cancer)
Ace of Cups
Ace: Ever-fresh happiness. A new connection or project that makes your heart overflow. Represents tapping into a constant source of joy and emotional renewal.
Two of Cups
Two: Romance and deep vibes. Represents a significant connection with another person or a healing relationship with yourself. A very positive omen for love and harmony.
Three of Cups
Three: Group joy and community. It's the "party" card. Connecting with friends, sharing happiness, and having a joyous occasion where you feel supported by a group.
Four of Cups
Four: Emotional break. Taking a "chill pill" because you've had too much external influence. It can also warn that you've been a recluse too long and are missing a new opportunity.
Five of Cups
Five: Sorrow and fixation. You are focused on the spilled cups (loss) and not seeing the two that are still full. A nudge to shift your perspective away from self-pity when you're ready.
Six of Cups
Six: Inner Child and nostalgia. Connecting with pure, innocent joy. A reminder to stay light-hearted and open your heart to fun connections like you did in childhood.
Seven of Cups
Seven: Choices and daydreams. Your head is in the clouds. Many opportunities seem real, but you must choose wisely and avoid looking at things through rose-colored glasses.
Eight of Cups
Eight: Seeking more. Walking away from current success or a situation because you need more emotional growth and a different perspective. A journey to find "that other cup."
Nine of Cups
Nine: Wish fulfillment. A miracle or a very pleasant surprise. Dream big and don't limit your happiness. You have enough right now to feel satisfied and full.
Ten of Cups
Ten: Emotional climax. Dreams coming true in love, family, and relationships. Success and total satisfaction on a soul level. A very long-term positive outcome.
Page of Cups
Page: Emotional messages. Could be a playful date offer or news that makes you very happy. Warning: don't be too immature or dramatic with your feelings.
Knight of Cups
Knight: The "College Romance" energy. More mature than the page, he seeks emotional depth and beauty. Slow down and get to know the beauty in life and the people around you.
Queen of Cups
Queen: Totally in tune with intuition. Represents psychic messages, dreams, and telepathic connections. Trust your heart and feel things out even if they don't make logical sense.
King of Cups
King: Mastery of the heart. He seems cold but feels deeply. He balances emotion with truth, making the best decision for the heart using both logic and love.

Suit of Pentacles

?
Pentacles (Earth Energy): Stability, work, money, and grounding. This suit is about our physical world, how we sustain ourselves, and our sense of security on the planet. (Signs: Virgo, Taurus, Capricorn)
Ace of Pentacles
Ace: New grounding. A slow-moving but very positive omen for your finances or sense of stability. Something is coming that aligns you with your physical self.
Two of Pentacles
Two: Balance. Managing your physical energy and balancing what you "need" to do vs what you "want" to do. Ground yourself to stop leaking energy through worry.
Three of Pentacles
Three: Collaboration. Group work where your efforts are highly regarded. If you put your work out there now, it will be well-rewarded and potentially bring financial gain.
Four of Pentacles
Four: Stability vs Greed. You have enough, but you might be clinging too tightly to a situation (or a person) that isn't doing you any good. Ask if you are actually aligned with what you're holding.
Five of Pentacles
Five: Lack mindset. Represents the "ebb" of the flow. You may feel like you don't have enough, or you are envious of others. Prepare for a phase where you need to be conservative.
Six of Pentacles
Six: Give and Take. Represents accumulated wisdom or financial stability. It's a sign to either offer your practical help to someone else or be humble enough to ask for it.
Seven of Pentacles
Seven: Patience. Your "crop" is growing. Slow down and appreciate how your efforts have paid off so far. Review the details to see if any further action is actually required.
Eight of Pentacles
Eight: Craftsmanship. Fulfillment through hard work. You are aligned with how you exchange energy with the world. Your efforts are giving back to you in a satisfying way.
Nine of Pentacles
Nine: Independent security. You are well-off and established. However, check if you are self-isolating too much—comfort is great, but don't stay in your shell forever.
Ten of Pentacles
Ten: Legacy and wealth. Whatever you are doing now has positive long-term consequences. It's a card of abundance in family, career, and future security.
Page of Pentacles
Page: New study. Someone starting a new trade or adventure. It's a sign to begin a new endeavor that will eventually lead to physical gratification.
Knight of Pentacles
Knight: Slow and steady. The most rational night. He is very dependable but works slowly. Don't rush the process; logical steps will get the job done eventually.
Queen of Pentacles
Queen: Nurturing Mother Earth. Urges you to take care of your body and your home. Enjoy the comforts of your physical life and use grounding to align yourself.
King of Pentacles
King: Master of stability. Be as practical as possible. Don't take risks right now; be conservative with your moves and listen to practical, expert advice.
